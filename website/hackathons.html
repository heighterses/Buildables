How Our Hackathons Work

From registration to demo day, here's what to expect

1

Register

Sign up individually or as a team through our online registration portal. No registration fee required.

2

Form Teams

Join existing teams or form new ones at our team formation events. Teams typically consist of 3-5 members.

3

Ideate

Brainstorm solutions to the hackathon challenge. Mentors are available to help refine your ideas.

4

Build

Develop your solution during the hackathon period, which typically ranges from 24 to 48 hours.

5

Present

Showcase your project to judges and fellow participants in a 5-minute presentation.

6

Win Prizes

Top projects receive recognition, prizes, and opportunities for further development support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our hackathons

Do I need to be an experienced programmer to participate?

Not at all! Our hackathons welcome participants of all skill levels. We encourage beginners to join and learn from the experience. Many teams benefit from diverse skills, including design, project management, and domain expertise.

Do I need to have a team before registering?

No, you can register individually and form a team during our team formation events. We provide opportunities for participants to connect and form teams based on shared interests and complementary skills.

What should I bring to a hackathon?

For in-person events, bring your laptop, charger, any specific hardware you might need for your project, and personal items for your comfort. For virtual events, ensure you have a stable internet connection and the necessary software installed.

Is there a registration fee?

Most of our hackathons are free to participate in, thanks to our sponsors and partners. Any exceptions will be clearly noted in the event details.

What happens after the hackathon?

Winning teams often receive prizes and opportunities for further development support. All participants are encouraged to continue working on their projects, and we provide resources and connections to help bring promising ideas to fruition.
